CHINCHOLI FOREST

Chincholi Forest The Chincholi forest is the first dry land Wildlife Sanctuary in South India. It is a reserved forest and is home to many diverse species of both animals and plants. The forest has many medicinal trees and plants including the Sandalwood tree. At the center of the forest there are 4 small dams that keep the core of the forest moist. It also has plantations of Acacia and Teak. The sanctuary is home to diverse creatures like the four horned antelope, fruit bat, Indian wolf, and the Black Buck. The forest is home to over 35 species of birds: Black drongo, Blue pigeon, Black-header Oriole, etc.
